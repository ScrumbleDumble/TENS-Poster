01 Motivation

After experiencing a tear to the meniscus and undergoing surgery, Clint needed rehabilitative physiotherapy to regain strength in his leg and prevent further muscular atrophy. In this process, a Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS) unit is used to stimulate the quadriceps so excessive stress is not applied to the meniscus — however, pain is still experienced in the joint due to daily locomotion.

02 Background

Osteoarthritis is the most common form of arthritis, affecting the entire joint including cartilage, bone and surrounding tissues [1]. It commonly causes pain, stiffness, swelling and reduced mobility, and the knee is one of the most frequently affected joints. Major risk factors include ageing, excess body weight, joint overuse and previous joint injury [2].

03 Current Situation

Existing TENS devices provide effective pain relief and support rehabilitation, but clinic units are bulky and difficult to transport. Patients struggle outside the clinic as relief wears off during daily activities.

Commercial portable TENS units exist for home use, but they apply a constant voltage on pre-set cycles — not adaptable in real time to the user's movement. Many are intended only for use at rest. Yet being sedentary for most of the day is not recommended, and patients must still move about.

04 Unmet Need

A portable, adaptable and user-friendly device that supports rehabilitation at home and on the go — one that responds to the body in motion, not just the body at rest.

The device must adapt its stimulation in real time, be wearable during daily locomotion, and integrate with the patient's everyday health ecosystem.

05 Proposed Solution

Research shows that using a TENS unit in tandem with intentional muscle activation reduces chronic and acute muscular pain, which in turn promotes recovery [3]. Using TENS during muscle activation helps reduce pain from arthritis — so a unit that stimulates the muscles as they contract during motion provides this solution. It can connect to personal health devices to sync to walking-cadence data, improving efficacy. This adaptable TENS unit is a suitable device to relieve pain and improve recovery for people who are still active and constantly on the move.
