01 Motivation

After a meniscus tear and subsequent surgery, Clint requires rehabilitative physiotherapy to regain leg strength and prevent further muscular atrophy. A TENS unit stimulates the quadriceps so that excessive stress is not transferred to the healing meniscus — yet persistent joint pain from daily locomotion remains.

02 Background

Osteoarthritis is the most common form of arthritis, affecting cartilage, bone and surrounding tissues. The knee is among the most frequently impacted joints, with ageing, excess weight, overuse and prior injury as leading risk factors.

03 Current Situation

Existing TENS devices deliver effective relief, but clinic-grade units are bulky and difficult to transport. Patients are tethered to the clinic, unable to receive support during the very daily activities that provoke their pain.

04 Unmet Need

A portable, adaptable and user-friendly device that supports rehabilitation at home and on the go — one that responds to the body in motion, not just the body at rest.

05 Proposed Solution

Consumer TENS units apply constant voltage on pre-set cycles. Research shows that pairing TENS with intentional muscle activation reduces chronic and acute muscular pain and accelerates recovery [1]. We propose a TENS unit that stimulates the muscles as they contract during motion — synchronised to walking cadence through paired personal health devices.
